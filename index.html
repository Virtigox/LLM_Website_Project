Introduction to LLMs

Large Language Models (LLMs) are a class of deep learning models designed to understand, generate, and manipulate human language. They are trained on large-scale datasets, such as text scraped from the internet, academic papers, novels, and articles, allowing them to capture core knowledge and patterns that reflect human language and society.

The Transformer Architecture

The foundation of modern LLMs is the Transformer architecture, which utilizes a mechanism called self-attention. This allows the model to weigh the importance of different words in the input text when processing any single word, dramatically improving its ability to understand context and long-range dependencies in language. This key innovation is what enabled the rapid advancement of generative AI.

    Open and proprietary ecosystems provide varied tradeoffs for customization, performance, and governance.

    Open-Source Models (Western):

    • Llama family: General-purpose models designed for fine-tuning on private data.
    • Mistral: Efficient text generation optimized for smaller hardware footprints.
    • Falcon: High-performing models released under permissive licenses for commercial reuse.

    Open-Source Models (Chinese):

    Chinese open-source models represented roughly 13% of global AI usage this year.
    • Qwen series: Dense and MoE variants with strong multilingual and multimodal support.
    • DeepSeek: Mixture-of-Experts architectures tuned for mathematical reasoning and coding.
    • Kimi series: Ultra-long 128K-token context windows with multimodal input support.

    Proprietary Models:

    • GPT-4 class: Broad general reasoning, coding, and creative capabilities.
    • Claude: Long-context analysis with safety-aligned dialogue and drafting.
    • Gemini: Multimodal workflows across text, imagery, and code.
